Blur Image Online — From Privacy Redaction to Artistic Effects

Sometimes you need to hide something in a photo — a license plate in a background, a name on a document, someone's face in a screenshot you're about to share online. Other times, you want something more artistic — a dreamy soft-focus look for a portrait, or motion blur to make a still image feel dynamic. This tool handles both ends of that spectrum.

There are four blur modes to pick from. Gaussian gives you that classic smooth soft-focus — great for softening backgrounds or reducing noise. Motion blur simulates camera shake along a direction you choose, which can add a sense of speed to action shots. Radial blur creates a zoom-burst effect from a center point you can position anywhere. And Pixelate turns areas into a chunky mosaic — the go-to option when you're censoring something and want it to be clearly unreadable.

How It Actually Works

Upload your image, pick a blur type, drag the intensity slider to taste, and hit Apply. You'll see the result right away before committing to a download. The whole thing runs in your browser using the Canvas API — no uploads, no server processing. Your images stay on your device from start to finish, which matters when you're working with sensitive screenshots or personal photos.

Picking the Right Blur

If you're trying to obscure private information — names, addresses, faces, plates — Pixelate at a high intensity is the way to go. It makes content clearly unreadable and looks intentional, not accidental. For aesthetic work, Gaussian at a lower setting gives a natural out-of-focus feel, like shallow depth of field from an expensive lens. Motion blur works well for creative edits where you want to fake movement, and Radial blur is your shortcut to that dramatic zoom effect photographers sometimes use.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I blur just part of the image, like a face?

This tool blurs the entire image uniformly. If you only need to blur one area, you could crop that section out, blur it separately, and layer it back in using a design tool. It's an extra step, but it works for quick redactions.

What's the difference between Gaussian blur and Pixelate?

Gaussian blur blends neighboring pixels into each other, creating a smooth, dreamy effect. Pixelate replaces groups of pixels with a single flat color, creating a mosaic look. Gaussian is better for artistic use; Pixelate is better when you need to clearly censor something.

Does blurring shrink the image or reduce resolution?

No — the output is the exact same resolution and dimensions as your original. Blurring changes how the pixels look, but it doesn't reduce the file's pixel count or dimensions at all.

Are my photos sent to a server?

No. Everything runs locally in your browser. The blur processing uses the Canvas API on your machine, so your images never leave your device and no data is transmitted anywhere.

Which blur should I use to censor sensitive info?

Pixelate at a high intensity is the standard for privacy redaction. It makes text, faces, and other details completely unrecognizable and looks deliberate — which is exactly what you want when sharing screenshots or photos publicly.
