What Is 2olhub, Exactly?

Think of it as a Swiss Army knife for your digital life. Need to merge three PDFs into one before a deadline? Done. Want to shrink a photo from 8MB to 500KB so it'll actually attach to an email? Takes about two seconds. Have a WebP image you downloaded from a website that your photo editor won't open? Convert it to JPG and move on with your day.

We built 2olhub because we got tired of the usual experience — you search for "merge PDF free," land on a site, and then it wants you to create an account, limits you to three files, or slaps a watermark on the output unless you pay. None of that here. The tools are free, they work in your browser, and there's no catch hiding behind a paywall.

How It Actually Works

Here's the thing most people don't realize — the majority of our tools run entirely in your browser. When you upload a file to compress an image or convert a PDF, it never actually leaves your computer. The processing happens right there on your device using JavaScript. That's why it feels so fast: there's no upload, no server queue, no waiting.

Who Uses This?

Honestly, a bit of everyone. Students use the AI writing tools when they need a first draft of an essay or a quick summary of a long article. Office workers merge contracts, compress reports that are too big for email, and password-protect sensitive documents before sending them to clients. A lot of that happens during the last five minutes before a deadline — we've been there.

Freelancers and small business owners probably use the image tools the most — resizing product photos for their Etsy shop, removing backgrounds for a clean white product shot, compressing 50 images at once so their website loads faster. And developers occasionally need things like Base64 encoding for an image or a quick HTML-to-PDF conversion for generating invoices. The point is, different people use different tools, and they're all here in one place.

We Don't Want Your Data

That probably sounds weird coming from a website, but it's true. Most of our tools process files directly in your browser — meaning your documents, images, and data literally never leave your device. We couldn't see them even if we wanted to, because the processing happens on your machine, not on our server.

A few tools need server-side processing — things like AI background removal and image enhancement, where we need to run a neural network that's too heavy for a browser. For those, your file is sent over an encrypted connection, processed in memory, and deleted the instant the result is ready. Nothing is logged, nothing is stored, nothing is shared. We built it this way because we'd want the same guarantee if we were the ones using these tools.
